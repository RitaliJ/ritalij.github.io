My broad academic interests lie in exploring the behavior and dynamics of complex systems. I'm interested in understanding how a simple set of rules or a group of properties can give rise to fascinating emergent phenomena — from molecular and neural systems to machine learning paradigms to economic and financial systems.

I seek to understand how perturbations or signals received in one component of a system can attenuate and yield larger change. Graph theory, network science, and signal processing provide a foundation for approaching such questions, alongside theoretical computer science and mathematics. I've employed these ideas in my theoretical molecular computing work in Professor Erik Winfree's DNA and Algorithms Lab at Caltech, where I've completed an independent project to understand how a self-assembling system might be capable of computing solutions to hard problems.
